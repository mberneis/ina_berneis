# Ina Berneis
Born: July 16, 1927 (Berlin, Germany)
Died: May 18, 2003 (New York, USA)
## Public Links
https://www.imdb.com/name/nm2339550

## Overview
Ina Berneis is a German photographer primarily known for her work in portraiture and still photography within the film and entertainment industries. Her career spans several decades, with significant contributions during the mid-20th century.

## Professional Style
Ina Berneis is recognized for her ability to capture intimate, candid moments of public figures, as well as formal studio-style portraits. Her background in still photography for cinema suggests a strong focus on capturing the atmosphere and narrative of a film set.

## Film Credits (Still Photographer)
Ina Berneis worked as a Still Photographer (Standfotografin) on the following productions. Her role was to capture scene stills for publicity and documentation during filming.

### Die bleierne Zeit (1981)

English Title: Marianne and Juliane (or The German Sisters)

Director: Margarethe von Trotta

Note: This is her most prominent credit in the New German Cinema era. She worked alongside fellow photographer Ralf Tooten.

https://www.filmportal.de/node/5517/gallery

### Nur zum Spaß, nur zum Spiel - Kaleidoskop Valeska Gert (1977)

English Title: Just for Fun, Just for Play

Director: Volker Schlöndorff

Note: A documentary about the legendary cabaret artist Valeska Gert. Ina's photos from this set are preserved in the Volker Schlöndorff Collection.

https://schloendorff.deutsches-filminstitut.de/sammlung/kaleidoskop-valeska-gert-fotos-werkfoto-68/

### Onkel Toms Hütte (1965)

English Title: Uncle Tom's Cabin

Ina is credited with the photography for the tie-in book published by Bertelsmann-Lesering in 1965. She was present on the set of the 1965 film adaptation directed by Géza von Radványi to capture production stills.

https://www.filmportal.de/node/44085/gallery

### Actor References (Photographed Subjects)
The following actors and public figures were subjects of Ina's photography, either on film sets or in exclusive portrait sessions.

#### Marlon Brando:
Ina was close friends and neighbors with Marlon Brando. - She took several portraits of him during the filming of ...

#### Christian Devi Brando: 
Ina took the first exclusive public photos of Marlon Brando's eldest son as a baby.

#### Anna Kashfi: 
Ina was a personal friend of Kashfi (Brando's first wife) and photographed her extensively during this period. These photos appeared in publications like Modern Screen.

#### Valeska Gert:
Ina captured candid and performance images of the eccentric dancer and pantomimist during the filming of the 1977 Schlöndorff documentary.
https://schloendorff.deutsches-filminstitut.de/sammlung/kaleidoskop-valeska-gert-fotos-werkfoto-68/

#### Pola Kinski:
Daughter of Klaus Kinski. Ina photographed her during the production of Kaleidoskop Valeska Gert (archived in the Schlöndorff Collection).

#### Oliver Tobias:
Swiss-born actor (known for The Stud). Ina shot portraits of him that were circulated as "Film Star Postcards" in Europe.

#### Jutta Lampe & Barbara Sukowa:
As the lead actresses in Die bleierne Zeit, they were the primary subjects of her unit photography for that film.

#### Ursula Andress
Ursula and Ina met in the 50's and became close friends for the whole lives.

### Professional Context
#### Archives: 
Her physical work (negatives and slides) is largely held in the Deutsches Filminstitut & Filmmuseum (DFF), specifically within the collections related to Volker Schlöndorff and New German Cinema.
https://schloendorff.deutsches-filminstitut.de/sammlung/kaleidoskop-valeska-gert-fotos-werkfoto-68/

#### Style: 
She bridged the gap between Hollywood glamour (late 50s) and the gritty, realistic style of New German Cinema (late 70s/80s).
